\hh{Anthropic vs. The President}
{\l{I}n an unexpected turn of events, Anthropic has found itself at odds with the President of the United States.
\par} 
The President has determined that Anthropic's Fable 5 \& Mythos 5 models pose a national security risk. In an effort to restrict access to these flagship systems, the President ordered Anthropic to block foreign nationals from using them.

That the President singled out a text-generation model is surprising, given the riskier systems already in circulation. xAI is in hot water over Grok's image tools: \href{https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/musks-ai-chatbot-grok-xai-making-sexual-deepfakes-imagine-rcna265855}{NBC News} found that Grok was still generating non-consensual sexualized deepfakes, \href{https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/27/grok-elon-musk-dutch-court-ban-ai-nudes.html}{CNBC} reported that a Dutch court ordered xAI to stop creating AI nudes, and \href{https://apnews.com/article/c1a3039e5aaeb4dd517d995b8b301537}{the AP} reported an EU investigation into images regulators said may amount to child sexual abuse material. The President did not intervene in that situation. Tesla's FSD may be more dangerous still, since its mistakes can happen while a vehicle is traveling at highway speed. That risk has already helped drive billions of dollars in litigation against the company.

But who cares if teenagers are testing out some `beta' self-driving technology. Age restricting that is too much work for regulators, and political leaders have more important things to do, like dropping bombs on Christmas. What is dangerous is this model by Anthropic that generates text. 

Sticks and stones and FSD may break my bones, but an LLM will never hurt me.

\hh{Fatal Autopilot Crash}
Yesterday, \href{https://electrek.co/2026/06/20/tesla-autopilot-katy-texas-home-crash-woman-killed/}{Electrek} reporter Fred Lambert published a report detailing a fatal crash in Katy, Texas. The crash involved a Tesla on autopilot which failed to make a right turn at an intersection, instead continuing forward and crashing directly into a home. A 76-year old woman inside the home was struck by the vehicle. She was flown to Memorial Hermann hospital, where she passed away.

On \href{https://www.king5.com/article/news/local/tesla-crashes-into-redmond-garage-after-reported-autopilot-malfunction/281-686c0c6a-0a5a-41ea-9675-550aeaffdd68}{June 3rd, 2026 in Panama City Beach, Florida}, one crashed into a public service building.

A longer \href{https://alshival.ai/publications/tesla-fsd/}{review of NHTSA complaint data} frames these crashes as part of a broader fixed-object pattern: tree, pole, wall, and building-impact complaints that often overlap with driver-assist language. That is exactly the point. If text generators are a national security problem, then vehicles alleged to drive into homes, poles, trees, ponds, and railroad gates deserve at least the same official curiosity.

\hh{The People hate Ai}
The public does not hate Ai because it is unfamiliar. They hate it because it is suddenly everywhere, expensive to run, hard to opt out of, and sold by people they do not trust.

According to \href{https://www.pewresearch.org/science/2025/09/17/how-americans-view-ai-and-its-impact-on-people-and-society/}{Pew Research Center}, half of U.S. adults are more concerned than excited about Ai in daily life, while only 10\% are more excited than concerned. Pew also found that 57\% of Americans rate Ai's societal risks as high, compared with 25\% who say the same about its benefits.

The workplace numbers are not much warmer. In another \href{https://www.pewresearch.org/social-trends/2025/02/25/u-s-workers-are-more-worried-than-hopeful-about-future-ai-use-in-the-workplace/}{Pew survey}, 52\% of workers said they were worried about future Ai use at work, while just 6\% thought it would create more job opportunities for them in the long run. It turns out people get nervous when every pitch deck says ``productivity'' and every layoff email says ``efficiency.''

The backlash is no longer theoretical. \href{https://news.gallup.com/poll/709772/americans-oppose-data-centers-area.aspx}{Gallup} found that 71\% of Americans oppose Ai data centers in their local area, including 48\% who are strongly opposed. Among opponents, half cited resource use such as water and electricity. That is the real politics of Ai: nobody wants the server farm, the power bill, or the chatbot boss, but everyone is supposed to clap for the future.

\href{https://www.axios.com/2025/12/10/americans-ai-use-worry-survey}{Axios}, reporting on Fathom polling, found that nearly seven in ten Americans have used Ai, but 49\% think it will create significant job loss. That may be the cleanest summary of the moment. The people are using Ai. They just do not believe the people selling it.
